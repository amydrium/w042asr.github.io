Grade 11

“ By learning these skills now, I will be better prepared for potentially pursuing 3D animation as a career in the future.”

Throughout this year, I have grown the most in my 3D modeling skills in Game Design class. I have spent the past three years learning 3D modeling, but this year, I have grown the most in my understanding of intermediate skills, such as rigging models, animation, and physics simulations in a program called Blender; additionally, I learned how to apply best practices to my work. By practicing 3D modeling in Game Design, I have also significantly decreased the amount of time I spend modeling—I now work more efficiently.

My first artifact is a gramophone I modeled in 9th grade for the Virtual History Museum project in US History class; I had little understanding of how 3D modeling worked at the time, and as a result, the model is of poor quality. Some of the most important best practices I have learned since creating this model include consistent topology and modeling in quads. Consistent topology means that each polygon in a model is roughly the same size, or at the very least, that the topology flows well. To model in quads is to only create models with rectangular faces; generally, other than quads, “tris” (triangular faces) are also acceptable, but they are generally avoided. Additionally, this model is made from the shape tools within Blender; it was not modeled by hand for the most part.

My second artifact is a model of an ottoman I made in tenth grade for a class demonstration in Game Design I. Though it is an improvement in many regards, it still is not my best work. I sculpted the creases in the fabric myself, but they look sloppy upon inspection, and the topology is still not fully consistent.

My third artifact is my most recent work in Game Design II. Starting in late third quarter, I began working individually to develop a portfolio showcasing my 3D modeling skills for use in college applications. This artifact is my first attempt at 3D animation; I created a walk cycle of a character I modeled in December last year. In working on this two-week long miniature project, I learned how to rig models for animation, how to use physics simulations to model clothing, and how to create simple looping animations. Throughout this process, I also learned a better method to UV unwrap models (preparing models for image textures.)

Overall, my growth in 3D modeling this year will help me in developing a work portfolio to use when applying to colleges next year. By learning these skills now, I will be better prepared for potentially pursuing 3D animation as a career in the future.
